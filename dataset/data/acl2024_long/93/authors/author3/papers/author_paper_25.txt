    "title": "A General Compressive Sensing Construct Using Density Evolution",
    "abstract": "This paper proposes a general framework to design a sparse sensing matrix <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {A}\\in \\mathbb{R}^{m\\times n}$</tex-math></inline-formula>, for a linear measurement system <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {y} = \\mathbf {Ax}^{\\natural } + \\mathbf {w}$</tex-math></inline-formula>, where <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {y} \\in \\mathbb{R}^{m}$</tex-math></inline-formula>, <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {x}^{\\natural }\\in \\mathbb{R}^{n}$</tex-math></inline-formula>, and <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {w}\\in \\mathbb{R}^{m}$</tex-math></inline-formula> denote the measurements, the signal with certain structures, and the measurement noise, respectively. By viewing the signal reconstruction from the measurements as a message passing algorithm over a graphical model, we leverage tools from coding theory, namely the density evolution technique, and provide a framework for the design of matrix <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\mathbf {A}$</tex-math></inline-formula>. Two design schemes for the sensing matrix, namely, <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$(i)$</tex-math></inline-formula> a regular sensing and <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$(ii)$</tex-math></inline-formula> a preferential sensing, are proposed and are incorporated into a single framework. As illustration, we consider the Lasso regression, ridge regression, and elastic net regression; and show that our framework can reproduce the classical results on the minimum sensor number, i.e., <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$m$</tex-math></inline-formula>. In the preferential sensing scenario, we consider the case in which the whole signal is divided into two disjoint parts, namely, high-priority part <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\boldsymbol{x}^{\\natural }_{\\mathsf {H}}$</tex-math></inline-formula> and low-priority part <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\boldsymbol{x}^{\\natural }_{\\mathsf {L}}$</tex-math></inline-formula>. Then, by formulating the sensing system design as a bi-convex optimization problem, we obtain sensing matrices which can provide a preferential treatment for <inline-formula><tex-math notation=\"LaTeX\">$\\boldsymbol{x}^{\\natural }_{\\mathsf {H}}$</tex-math></inline-formula>. Numerical experiments with both synthetic data and real-world data are also provided to verify the effectiveness of our design scheme."
