    "Idea": "This research introduces **anomaly-driven persona prompting**, a novel approach that leverages annotator disagreements as a signal for improving LLM simulations. The key insight is that annotator disagreements often reflect meaningful variations in perspective that are not captured by traditional persona variables. The approach involves identifying cases of high annotator disagreement, analyzing the underlying reasons for these disagreements (e.g., cultural, contextual, or situational factors), and using this analysis to design targeted persona prompts. These prompts are then used to fine-tune LLMs, enabling them to simulate a wider range of perspectives. The research will also develop a new evaluation metric, **Disagreement-Aware F1 (DA-F1)**, which measures how well LLMs capture the diversity of perspectives in cases of annotator disagreement. The approach will be tested on existing subjective NLP datasets, such as EPIC and POPQUORN, as well as a new dataset, **DisagreeSim**, which focuses on high-disagreement cases.",
